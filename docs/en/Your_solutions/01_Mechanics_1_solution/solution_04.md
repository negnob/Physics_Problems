## 4. Vector Calculus

The position vector is

$$\vec r(t)=(3t^2)\hat i+(5t-8t^2)\hat j$$

We need to find velocity and acceleration.

---

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

Velocity is the derivative of position:

$$\vec v(t)=\frac{d\vec r}{dt}$$

Acceleration is the derivative of velocity:

$$\vec a(t)=\frac{d\vec v}{dt}=\frac{d^2\vec r}{dt^2}$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1. Differentiate to find velocity

$$\vec r(t)=(3t^2)\hat i+(5t-8t^2)\hat j$$

Differentiate each component:

$$\frac{d}{dt}(3t^2)=6t$$

$$\frac{d}{dt}(5t-8t^2)=5-16t$$

So

$$\boxed{\vec v(t)=6t\hat i+(5-16t)\hat j}$$

---

### Step 2. Differentiate again to find acceleration

Differentiate velocity:

$$\frac{d}{dt}(6t)=6$$

$$\frac{d}{dt}(5-16t)=-16$$

So

$$\boxed{\vec a(t)=6\hat i-16\hat j}$$

---

## Final Answer

$$\boxed{\vec v(t)=6t\hat i+(5-16t)\hat j}$$

$$\boxed{\vec a(t)=6\hat i-16\hat j}$$
