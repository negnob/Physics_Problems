## 5. Relative Velocity

A river flows east at

$$2\text{ m/s}$$

A boat can travel at

$$5\text{ m/s}$$

in still water and wants to go directly north across the river.

The river is

$$200\text{ m}$$

wide.

---

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

The ground velocity is the sum of:

$$\vec v_{\text{ground}}=\vec v_{\text{boat/water}}+\vec v_{\text{river}}$$

To go directly north, the east-west component of the ground velocity must be zero.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1. Cancel the river flow

Let the boat head west of north by angle $\theta$.

Its westward component must cancel the river's eastward speed:

$$5\sin\theta=2$$

So

$$\sin\theta=\frac{2}{5}$$

$$\theta=\sin^{-1}\left(\frac{2}{5}\right)\approx23.58^\circ$$

So the boat should head

$$\boxed{23.58^\circ\text{ west of north}}$$

---

### Step 2. Find the northward speed

The northward component is

$$v_{\text{north}}=5\cos\theta$$

Since

$$\cos\theta=\sqrt{1-\sin^2\theta}=\sqrt{1-\left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2}=\frac{\sqrt{21}}{5}$$

we get

$$v_{\text{north}}=5\cdot\frac{\sqrt{21}}{5}=\sqrt{21}\text{ m/s}$$

So the crossing speed is

$$\sqrt{21}\text{ m/s}$$

---

### Step 3. Find the crossing time

$$t=\frac{\text{distance}}{\text{speed}}=\frac{200}{\sqrt{21}}$$

$$t\approx43.64\text{ s}$$

---

## Final Answer

The boat should head

$$\boxed{23.58^\circ\text{ west of north}}$$

The time to cross is

$$\boxed{\frac{200}{\sqrt{21}}\text{ s}\approx43.64\text{ s}}$$
