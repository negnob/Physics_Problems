## 8. Circular Motion

Calculate the centripetal acceleration of a person standing on the Earth's equator.

The Earth's radius is approximately

$$R=6378\text{ km}$$

---

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

A person standing on the equator moves in a circle because the Earth rotates.

The centripetal acceleration for circular motion is

$$a_c=\frac{v^2}{R}$$

It can also be written as

$$a_c=\omega^2R$$

where:
- $R$ is the radius of the circular path
- $v$ is the linear speed
- $\omega$ is the angular speed

The angular speed is

$$\omega=\frac{2\pi}{T}$$

where $T$ is the period of rotation.

For the Earth, one full rotation takes about

$$T=24\cdot3600=86400\text{ s}$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1. Convert the radius into meters

The radius is given in kilometers:

$$R=6378\text{ km}$$

Convert to meters:

$$R=6378\cdot1000=6.378\times10^6\text{ m}$$

---

### Step 2. Find the angular speed of the Earth

Use

$$\omega=\frac{2\pi}{T}$$

Substitute

$$T=86400\text{ s}$$

So

$$\omega=\frac{2\pi}{86400}$$

$$\omega\approx7.27\times10^{-5}\text{ rad/s}$$

---

### Step 3. Use the centripetal acceleration formula

Now use

$$a_c=\omega^2R$$

Substitute the values:

$$a_c=\left(\frac{2\pi}{86400}\right)^2\cdot6.378\times10^6$$

First square the angular speed:

$$\omega^2\approx(7.27\times10^{-5})^2\approx5.29\times10^{-9}$$

Now multiply by the radius:

$$a_c\approx(5.29\times10^{-9})(6.378\times10^6)$$

$$a_c\approx0.0337\text{ m/s}^2$$

---

## Final Answer

The centripetal acceleration of a person standing on the Earth's equator is

$$\boxed{a_c\approx0.0337\text{ m/s}^2}$$
