## 8. Circular Motion

We need the centripetal acceleration of a person standing on the Earth's equator.

Earth's radius:

$$R=6378\text{ km}=6.378\times10^6\text{ m}$$

---

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

For circular motion,

$$a_c=\omega^2R$$

where $\omega$ is angular speed.

The Earth rotates once every

$$T=24\cdot3600=86400\text{ s}$$

So

$$\omega=\frac{2\pi}{T}$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1. Find angular speed

$$\omega=\frac{2\pi}{86400}$$

---

### Step 2. Use the centripetal acceleration formula

$$a_c=\omega^2R=\left(\frac{2\pi}{86400}\right)^2(6.378\times10^6)$$

Numerically,

$$a_c\approx0.0337\text{ m/s}^2$$

---

## Final Answer

The centripetal acceleration at the equator is

$$\boxed{a_c\approx0.0337\text{ m/s}^2}$$
