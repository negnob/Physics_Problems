## 1. Wave Properties

### Useful definitions and formulas

For a wave, the basic relation is:

$$v=f\lambda$$

This means:

- $v$ is the wave speed
- $f$ is the frequency
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength

If we want to find wavelength, we rearrange the formula:

$$\lambda=\frac{v}{f}$$

This means:
- we divide the speed by the frequency
- because wavelength tells us how long one wave is
- if the wave moves faster while frequency stays the same, the wavelength becomes bigger

Important idea:

When a sound wave goes from air into water:
- the **frequency does not change**
- the **speed changes**
- so the **wavelength changes**

---

### Step-by-step solution

We are given:

- $f=440\text{ Hz}$
- speed in air: $v_{\text{air}}=343\text{ m/s}$
- speed in water: $v_{\text{water}}=1482\text{ m/s}$

---

### 1) Find the wavelength in air

Use the formula:

$$\lambda=\frac{v}{f}$$

For air:

$$\lambda_{\text{air}}=\frac{343}{440}$$

Now divide:

$$\lambda_{\text{air}}=0.7795\text{ m}$$

Rounded:

$$\lambda_{\text{air}}\approx0.78\text{ m}$$

Explanation:

- we took the speed in air
- divided it by the frequency
- that gives the length of one wave in air

---

### 2) Find the wavelength in water

Use the same formula:

$$\lambda=\frac{v}{f}$$

For water:

$$\lambda_{\text{water}}=\frac{1482}{440}$$

Now divide:

$$\lambda_{\text{water}}=3.368\text{ m}$$

Rounded:

$$\lambda_{\text{water}}\approx3.37\text{ m}$$

Explanation:

- the frequency is still the same: $440\text{ Hz}$
- but sound moves much faster in water
- so the wavelength becomes much larger

---

### Final answer

$$\lambda_{\text{air}}\approx0.78\text{ m}$$

$$\lambda_{\text{water}}\approx3.37\text{ m}$$
