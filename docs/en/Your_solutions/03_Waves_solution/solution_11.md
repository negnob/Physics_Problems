## 11. Animation: Two-Slit Interference

### Useful definitions and formulas

The total wave is the sum of the two waves from two slits:

$$u(\vec{r},t)=\frac{A}{|\vec{r}-\vec{r_1}|}\sin(k|\vec{r}-\vec{r_1}|-\omega t)+\frac{A}{|\vec{r}-\vec{r_2}|}\sin(k|\vec{r}-\vec{r_2}|-\omega t)$$

where:

- $\vec{r}$ is the observation point
- $\vec{r_1}$ is the position of the first slit
- $\vec{r_2}$ is the position of the second slit
- $A$ is amplitude
- $k=\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$ is wave number
- $\omega=2\pi f$ is angular frequency

The slit separation is:

$$d=|\vec{r_1}-\vec{r_2}|$$

Important idea:

- waves from the two slits meet
- sometimes they add and make a bright region
- sometimes they cancel and make a dark region
- this creates an interference pattern
