## 4. Phase Difference

### Useful definitions and formulas

The phase difference between two points on a wave separated by distance $\Delta x$ is:

$$\Delta\phi=\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\Delta x$$

where:

- $\Delta\phi$ is the phase difference in radians
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength
- $\Delta x$ is the distance between the points

Explanation of the formula:

- one full wavelength corresponds to a phase change of $2\pi$
- if the distance is only part of a wavelength, then the phase difference is the same fraction of $2\pi$

---

### Step-by-step solution

We are told that the two points are separated by:

$$\Delta x=\frac{\lambda}{3}$$

Now use:

$$\Delta\phi=\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\Delta x$$

Substitute:

$$\Delta\phi=\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\cdot\frac{\lambda}{3}$$

Now cancel $\lambda$ in the numerator and denominator:

$$\Delta\phi=\frac{2\pi}{3}$$

---

### Final answer

$$\Delta\phi=\frac{2\pi}{3}\text{ rad}$$
