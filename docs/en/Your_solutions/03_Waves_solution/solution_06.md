## 6. Wave Equation

### Useful definitions and formulas

The given wave is:

$$y(x,t)=0.05\sin(2\pi x-50\pi t)$$

The standard form of a traveling wave is:

$$y(x,t)=A\sin(kx-\omega t)$$

where:

- $A$ is amplitude
- $k$ is wave number
- $\omega$ is angular frequency

Useful formulas:

$$k=\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$$

$$\omega=2\pi f$$

$$v=f\lambda$$

Also:

$$v=\frac{\omega}{k}$$

---

### Step-by-step solution

Compare the given equation:

$$y(x,t)=0.05\sin(2\pi x-50\pi t)$$

with the standard form:

$$y(x,t)=A\sin(kx-\omega t)$$

By comparison:

- $A=0.05$
- $k=2\pi$
- $\omega=50\pi$

Now find each quantity one by one.

---

### a) Amplitude $A$

The amplitude is the number in front of the sine:

$$A=0.05\text{ m}$$

Explanation:

- amplitude is the maximum displacement from equilibrium
- here the wave can go up to $0.05\text{ m}$ and down to $-0.05\text{ m}$

---

### b) Wavelength $\lambda$

Use:

$$k=\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$$

Now solve for $\lambda$:

$$\lambda=\frac{2\pi}{k}$$

Substitute $k=2\pi$:

$$\lambda=\frac{2\pi}{2\pi}$$

$$\lambda=1\text{ m}$$

Explanation:

- the wave number tells us how quickly the wave changes in space
- larger $k$ means shorter wavelength
- here it gives exactly $1\text{ m}$

---

### c) Frequency $f$

Use:

$$\omega=2\pi f$$

Now solve for $f$:

$$f=\frac{\omega}{2\pi}$$

Substitute $\omega=50\pi$:

$$f=\frac{50\pi}{2\pi}$$

Cancel $\pi$:

$$f=\frac{50}{2}$$

$$f=25\text{ Hz}$$

Explanation:

- angular frequency is related to ordinary frequency
- dividing by $2\pi$ converts it to frequency in hertz

---

### d) Wave speed $v$

Use:

$$v=f\lambda$$

Substitute:

$$v=25\cdot1$$

$$v=25\text{ m/s}$$

We can also check with:

$$v=\frac{\omega}{k}=\frac{50\pi}{2\pi}=25\text{ m/s}$$

Same answer.

---

### Final answer

a) $$A=0.05\text{ m}$$

b) $$\lambda=1\text{ m}$$

c) $$f=25\text{ Hz}$$

d) $$v=25\text{ m/s}$$
