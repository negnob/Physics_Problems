## 5. Echo Ranging

### Useful definitions and formulas

The general motion formula is:

$$d=vt$$

where:

- $d$ is distance
- $v$ is speed
- $t$ is time

For an echo:
- the sound goes **to the cliff**
- then it comes **back**

So the total distance traveled by sound is:

$$2x=vt$$

where $x$ is the one-way distance to the cliff

To find $x$, divide both sides by $2$:

$$x=\frac{vt}{2}$$

---

### Step-by-step solution

We are given:

- $t=1\text{ s}$
- $v=343\text{ m/s}$

Use:

$$x=\frac{vt}{2}$$

Substitute:

$$x=\frac{343\cdot1}{2}$$

$$x=\frac{343}{2}$$

$$x=171.5\text{ m}$$

Explanation:

- in 1 second, the sound traveled all the way there and back
- so that total path is $343\text{ m}$
- but the cliff is only half of that distance away

---

### Final answer

$$x=171.5\text{ m}$$

The cliff is **171.5 m away**.
