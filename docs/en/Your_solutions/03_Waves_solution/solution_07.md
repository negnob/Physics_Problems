## 7. Standing Wave Modes

### Useful definitions and formulas

For a string fixed at both ends, the standing wave condition is:

$$L=n\frac{\lambda}{2}$$

where:

- $L$ is the string length
- $\lambda$ is the wavelength
- $n$ is the harmonic number (mode number)

---

### Important idea (very important)

In a standing wave on a string:

- the ends of the string are **fixed**, so they are always **nodes**
- between nodes, the string forms **antinodes** (points of maximum motion)

Key fact:

- **one antinode corresponds to half of a wavelength** ($\lambda/2$)

So:

- if we count the number of antinodes, we know how many $\frac{\lambda}{2}$ segments fit into the string

That is why:

$$L=n\frac{\lambda}{2}$$

---

### Step-by-step solution

We are given:

- $L=80\text{ cm}=0.80\text{ m}$
- number of antinodes = $4$

---

### 1) Determine harmonic number

Since:

- each antinode = one half-wavelength
- and we have 4 antinodes

So:

$$n=4$$

Explanation:

- the string is divided into 4 equal vibrating sections
- each section is $\frac{\lambda}{2}$

---

### 2) Substitute into the formula

$$L=n\frac{\lambda}{2}$$

Substitute values:

$$0.80=4\cdot\frac{\lambda}{2}$$

Explanation:

- we replace $L$ with $0.80$
- we replace $n$ with $4$
- $\lambda$ is what we want to find

---

### 3) Simplify the expression

$$4\cdot\frac{\lambda}{2}=2\lambda$$

So:

$$0.80=2\lambda$$

Explanation:

- $4/2=2$, so the expression becomes $2\lambda$
- now the equation is simpler

---

### 4) Solve for $\lambda$

Divide both sides by 2:

$$\lambda=\frac{0.80}{2}$$

$$\lambda=0.40\text{ m}$$

---

### 5) Convert to centimeters

$$\lambda=40\text{ cm}$$

---

### 6) Physical meaning (очень полезно сказать)

- the total string length contains 4 half-wavelengths
- so in total, the string contains 2 full wavelengths
- that is why the wavelength is smaller than the string length

---

### Final answer

$$\lambda=0.40\text{ m}$$

or

$$\lambda=40\text{ cm}$$
