## 7. Standard Deviation

### Useful definitions and formulas

Mean value:

$$\bar{x}=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}x_i$$

Sample standard deviation:

$$\sigma=\sqrt{\frac{1}{N-1}\sum_{i=1}^{N}(x_i-\bar{x})^2}$$

where:

$\bar{x}$ — mean score  
$N$ — number of scores  
$x_i$ — each score  
$\sigma$ — standard deviation  

---

### Step-by-step solution

Given scores:

$$88,92,79,85,95,81,86,90,83,77,89$$

Number of scores:

$$N=11$$

Calculate the mean:

$$\bar{x}=\frac{88+92+79+85+95+81+86+90+83+77+89}{11}$$

$$\bar{x}=\frac{945}{11}$$

$$\bar{x}\approx85.91$$

Now calculate the sample standard deviation:

$$\sigma\approx5.58$$

So, for all 11 scores:

$$\bar{x}\approx85.91$$

$$\sigma\approx5.58$$

---

Now remove the highest and lowest scores.

Lowest score:

$$77$$

Highest score:

$$95$$

Remaining scores:

$$79,81,83,85,86,88,89,90,92$$

Number of remaining scores:

$$N=9$$

New mean:

$$\bar{x}=\frac{79+81+83+85+86+88+89+90+92}{9}$$

$$\bar{x}=\frac{773}{9}$$

$$\bar{x}\approx85.89$$

New sample standard deviation:

$$\sigma\approx4.31$$

Final answer:

For all scores:

$$\bar{x}=85.91,\quad\sigma=5.58$$

After removing the highest and lowest scores:

$$\bar{x}=85.89,\quad\sigma=4.31$$

Removing the highest and lowest scores made the standard deviation smaller, because the data became less spread out.
