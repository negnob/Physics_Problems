# Section 7: Measurements

## 1. Propagation of Error I

### Useful definitions and formulas

Volume of a sphere:

$$V=\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$$

Uncertainty propagation:

$$\Delta V=\left|\frac{dV}{dr}\right|\Delta r$$

For a sphere:

$$\Delta V=4\pi r^2\Delta r$$

where:

$r$ — radius  
$\Delta r$ — uncertainty of radius  
$V$ — volume  
$\Delta V$ — uncertainty of volume  

---

### Step-by-step solution

Given:

$$r=6.20\text{ cm}$$

$$\Delta r=0.05\text{ cm}$$

First calculate the volume:

$$V=\frac{4}{3}\pi(6.20)^3$$

$$V\approx998.31\text{ cm}^3$$

Now calculate the uncertainty:

$$\Delta V=4\pi(6.20)^2(0.05)$$

$$\Delta V\approx24.15\text{ cm}^3$$

Final answer:

$$V=(998\pm24)\text{ cm}^3$$

So, the volume of the sphere is approximately $998\text{ cm}^3$, with an uncertainty of about $24\text{ cm}^3$.
