## 3. Propagation of Error III

### Useful definitions and formulas

Ohm's Law:

$$R=\frac{V}{I}$$

For division, relative uncertainties are combined like this:

$$\frac{\Delta R}{R}=\sqrt{\left(\frac{\Delta V}{V}\right)^2+\left(\frac{\Delta I}{I}\right)^2}$$

Then:

$$\Delta R=R\sqrt{\left(\frac{\Delta V}{V}\right)^2+\left(\frac{\Delta I}{I}\right)^2}$$

where:

$R$ — resistance  
$V$ — voltage  
$I$ — current  
$\Delta R$ — uncertainty of resistance  

---

### Step-by-step solution

Given:

$$V=10.0\text{ V}$$

$$\Delta V=0.2\text{ V}$$

$$I=2.00\text{ A}$$

$$\Delta I=0.05\text{ A}$$

First calculate the resistance:

$$R=\frac{10.0}{2.00}$$

$$R=5.00\Omega$$

Now calculate the uncertainty:

$$\Delta R=5.00\sqrt{\left(\frac{0.2}{10.0}\right)^2+\left(\frac{0.05}{2.00}\right)^2}$$

$$\Delta R\approx0.16\Omega$$

Final answer:

$$R=(5.00\pm0.16)\Omega$$

So, the resistance is $5.00\Omega$, with an uncertainty of about $0.16\Omega$.
