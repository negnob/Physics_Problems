## 4. Relative Uncertainty

### Useful definitions and formulas

Percentage uncertainty means:

$$\Delta v=\frac{p}{100}\cdot v$$

where:

$v$ — measured value  
$p$ — percentage uncertainty  
$\Delta v$ — absolute uncertainty  

The actual value is in the range:

$$v-\Delta v\leq v_{actual}\leq v+\Delta v$$

---

### Step-by-step solution

Given:

$$v=60\text{ km/h}$$

$$p=5\%$$

Calculate the uncertainty:

$$\Delta v=\frac{5}{100}\cdot60$$

$$\Delta v=3\text{ km/h}$$

Now calculate the range:

$$60-3=57\text{ km/h}$$

$$60+3=63\text{ km/h}$$

Final answer:

$$57\text{ km/h}\leq v_{actual}\leq63\text{ km/h}$$

So, the car's actual speed is between $57\text{ km/h}$ and $63\text{ km/h}$.
