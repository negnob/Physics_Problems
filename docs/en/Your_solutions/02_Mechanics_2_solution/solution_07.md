## 7. Dynamics with Friction

A $5\text{ kg}$ block is on a $10\text{ kg}$ block. A force of $45\text{ N}$ is applied to the $10\text{ kg}$ block. The $5\text{ kg}$ block is tied to the wall. The coefficient of kinetic friction for all moving surfaces is

$$\mu_k=0.2$$

We need the acceleration of the $10\text{ kg}$ block.

---

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

Kinetic friction:

$$f_k=\mu_kN$$

Newton's second law:

$$F_{\text{net}}=ma$$

The $10\text{ kg}$ block experiences two friction forces opposing motion:

- friction with the ground
- friction with the top block

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

Let the lower block be $M=10\text{ kg}$ and the top block be $m=5\text{ kg}$.

### Step 1. Friction between upper and lower block

Normal force from upper block:

$$N_1=mg=5(9.8)=49\text{ N}$$

So friction there is

$$f_1=\mu_kN_1=0.2(49)=9.8\text{ N}$$

This friction acts left on the $10\text{ kg}$ block.

---

### Step 2. Friction between lower block and ground

The ground supports both masses, so normal force is

$$N_2=(M+m)g=(10+5)(9.8)=147\text{ N}$$

Thus ground friction is

$$f_2=\mu_kN_2=0.2(147)=29.4\text{ N}$$

Also acting left.

---

### Step 3. Net force on the $10\text{ kg}$ block

Applied force is $45\text{ N}$ to the right.

So net force is

$$F_{\text{net}}=45-9.8-29.4=5.8\text{ N}$$

---

### Step 4. Acceleration

$$a=\frac{F_{\text{net}}}{M}=\frac{5.8}{10}=0.58\text{ m/s}^2$$

---

## Final Answer

The acceleration of the $10\text{ kg}$ block is

$$\boxed{0.58\text{ m/s}^2}$$
