## 5. Inelastic Collision

A runner of mass

$$m_1=70\text{ kg}$$

moves at

$$v_1=3\text{ m/s}$$

and jumps onto a stationary cart of mass

$$m_2=140\text{ kg}$$

We need:
- the final speed
- whether kinetic energy is conserved

---

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

For a perfectly inelastic collision, momentum is conserved:

$$m_1v_1+m_2v_2=(m_1+m_2)v_f$$

But kinetic energy is generally **not** conserved in an inelastic collision.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1. Find final speed

Since the cart is initially at rest, $v_2=0$.

So:

$$70\cdot3+140\cdot0=(70+140)v_f$$

$$210=210v_f$$

$$v_f=1\text{ m/s}$$

---

### Step 2. Check kinetic energy

Initial kinetic energy:

$$K_i=\frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2=\frac{1}{2}(70)(3^2)=35\cdot9=315\text{ J}$$

Final kinetic energy:

$$K_f=\frac{1}{2}(m_1+m_2)v_f^2=\frac{1}{2}(210)(1^2)=105\text{ J}$$

Since

$$105\neq315$$

kinetic energy is not conserved.

---

## Final Answer

Final speed:

$$\boxed{v_f=1.0\text{ m/s}}$$

Kinetic energy is **not** conserved.

It decreases from

$$315\text{ J}$$

to

$$105\text{ J}$$

because some energy is transformed into heat, sound, and deformation.
