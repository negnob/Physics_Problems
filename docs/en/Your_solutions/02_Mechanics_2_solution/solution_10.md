## 10. Force Field and Power

Given

$$m=0.5\text{ kg}$$

and

$$x=5t^2-t,\qquad y=2t^3,\qquad z=-3t+2$$

We need:
- velocity
- momentum
- acceleration
- force
- power

---

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

Velocity:

$$\vec v=\frac{d\vec r}{dt}$$

Momentum:

$$\vec p=m\vec v$$

Acceleration:

$$\vec a=\frac{d\vec v}{dt}$$

Force:

$$\vec F=m\vec a$$

Power:

$$P=\vec F\cdot\vec v$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1. Position vector

$$\vec r(t)=\left(5t^2-t,\;2t^3,\;-3t+2\right)$$

---

### Step 2. Velocity

Differentiate each component:

$$\vec v(t)=\left(10t-1,\;6t^2,\;-3\right)$$

---

### Step 3. Momentum

$$\vec p(t)=m\vec v(t)=0.5\left(10t-1,\;6t^2,\;-3\right)$$

So

$$\vec p(t)=\left(5t-0.5,\;3t^2,\;-1.5\right)$$

---

### Step 4. Acceleration

Differentiate velocity:

$$\vec a(t)=\left(10,\;12t,\;0\right)$$

---

### Step 5. Force

$$\vec F(t)=m\vec a(t)=0.5(10,\;12t,\;0)=(5,\;6t,\;0)$$

---

### Step 6. Power

$$P=\vec F\cdot\vec v$$

$$P=(5,6t,0)\cdot(10t-1,6t^2,-3)$$

$$P=5(10t-1)+6t(6t^2)+0$$

$$P=50t-5+36t^3$$

So

$$\boxed{P(t)=36t^3+50t-5}$$

---

## Final Answer

$$\boxed{\vec v(t)=\left(10t-1,\;6t^2,\;-3\right)}$$

$$\boxed{\vec p(t)=\left(5t-0.5,\;3t^2,\;-1.5\right)}$$

$$\boxed{\vec a(t)=\left(10,\;12t,\;0\right)}$$

$$\boxed{\vec F(t)=\left(5,\;6t,\;0\right)}$$

$$\boxed{P(t)=36t^3+50t-5}$$
