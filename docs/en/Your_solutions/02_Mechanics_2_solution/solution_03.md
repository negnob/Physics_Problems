## 3. Conservation of Energy

A pendulum has length

$$L=1.0\text{ m}$$

and is released from angle

$$15^\circ$$

We need the speed at the bottom.

---

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

Mechanical energy is conserved:

$$mgh=\frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

For a pendulum released from angle $\theta$,

the vertical drop is

$$h=L-L\cos\theta=L(1-\cos\theta)$$

Then

$$v=\sqrt{2gh}$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1. Find the vertical drop

Use

$$h=L(1-\cos\theta)$$

Substitute

$$L=1.0,\qquad\theta=15^\circ$$

$$h=1(1-\cos15^\circ)$$

Since

$$\cos15^\circ\approx0.9659$$

we get

$$h\approx1-0.9659=0.0341\text{ m}$$

---

### Step 2. Use energy conservation

$$mgh=\frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

Mass cancels:

$$gh=\frac{1}{2}v^2$$

$$v=\sqrt{2gh}$$

Substitute:

$$v=\sqrt{2(9.8)(0.0341)}$$

$$v\approx\sqrt{0.668}\approx0.817\text{ m/s}$$

---

## Final Answer

The speed at the bottom is

$$\boxed{v\approx0.82\text{ m/s}}$$
