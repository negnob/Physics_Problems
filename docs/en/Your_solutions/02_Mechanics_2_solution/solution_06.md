## 6. Energy Dissipation

A tennis ball is dropped from height

$$h_0=2.0\text{ m}$$

After each bounce, it loses $30\%$ of its mechanical energy.

We need the height after the second bounce.

---

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

If the ball loses $30\%$ of its energy, then it keeps

$$70\%=0.7$$

of its energy after each bounce.

Since gravitational potential energy is proportional to height,

$$E=mgh$$

the new height is multiplied by the same factor:

$$h_{\text{new}}=0.7h_{\text{old}}$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1. Height after first bounce

$$h_1=0.7h_0=0.7(2.0)=1.4\text{ m}$$

---

### Step 2. Height after second bounce

$$h_2=0.7h_1=0.7(1.4)=0.98\text{ m}$$

---

## Final Answer

The ball rises after the second bounce to

$$\boxed{0.98\text{ m}}$$
