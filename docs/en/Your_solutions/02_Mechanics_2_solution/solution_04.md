## 4. Energy & Momentum

A block of mass

$$m_1=0.5\text{ kg}$$

slides down from height

$$h=3.0\text{ m}$$

and collides inelastically with a stationary block

$$m_2=1.5\text{ kg}$$

They stick together. We need the speed just after collision.

---

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

Before collision, gravitational potential energy becomes kinetic energy:

$$mgh=\frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

So speed before collision is

$$v=\sqrt{2gh}$$

For the collision, momentum is conserved:

$$m_1v_1+m_2v_2=(m_1+m_2)v_f$$

Since the second block is initially at rest, $v_2=0$.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1. Speed of the first block at the bottom

$$v_1=\sqrt{2gh}=\sqrt{2(9.8)(3.0)}$$

$$v_1=\sqrt{58.8}\approx7.67\text{ m/s}$$

---

### Step 2. Use conservation of momentum

Since they stick together,

$$m_1v_1=(m_1+m_2)v_f$$

Substitute values:

$$0.5(7.67)=(0.5+1.5)v_f$$

$$3.835=2.0v_f$$

$$v_f=1.9175\text{ m/s}$$

---

## Final Answer

The speed of the combined mass just after collision is

$$\boxed{v_f\approx1.92\text{ m/s}}$$
