# 9. Refraction — Snell's Law

## Useful definitions and formulas

Snell's Law:

$$n_1\sin\theta_1=n_2\sin\theta_2$$

where:

$n_1$ — refractive index of first medium  
$n_2$ — refractive index of second medium  
$\theta_1$ — angle of incidence  
$\theta_2$ — angle of refraction  

## Step-by-step solution

Given:

$$n_1=1.00$$

$$n_2=1.50$$

$$\theta_1=30^\circ$$

Use Snell's Law:

$$1.00\sin30^\circ=1.50\sin\theta_2$$

Since:

$$\sin30^\circ=0.5$$

Then:

$$0.5=1.50\sin\theta_2$$

$$\sin\theta_2=\frac{0.5}{1.50}$$

$$\sin\theta_2=0.333$$

$$\theta_2=\sin^{-1}(0.333)$$

$$\theta_2=19.5^\circ$$

## Final answer

$$\theta_2=19.5^\circ$$

The ray bends toward the normal because glass has a higher refractive index than air.
