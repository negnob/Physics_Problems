# 6. EM Wave Analysis

## Useful definitions and formulas

General form of an electromagnetic wave:

$$E=E_0\sin(kx-\omega t)$$

If the wave has $kx-\omega t$, it moves in the positive $x$ direction.

Wavelength:

$$\lambda=\frac{2\pi}{k}$$

Angular frequency in vacuum:

$$\omega=ck$$

Magnetic field amplitude:

$$B_0=\frac{E_0}{c}$$

where:

$c=3.0\times10^8\,m/s$

## Step-by-step solution

Given:

$$E_y(x,t)=100\sin(10^7x-\omega t)$$

So:

$$E_0=100\,V/m$$

$$k=10^7\,m^{-1}$$

### Direction of propagation

Because the wave has the form:

$$kx-\omega t$$

it propagates in the positive $x$ direction.

### Wavelength

$$\lambda=\frac{2\pi}{10^7}$$

$$\lambda=6.28\times10^{-7}\,m$$

### Angular frequency

$$\omega=ck$$

$$\omega=3.0\times10^8\cdot10^7$$

$$\omega=3.0\times10^{15}\,rad/s$$

### Magnetic field

$$B_0=\frac{100}{3.0\times10^8}$$

$$B_0=3.33\times10^{-7}\,T$$

Since $E$ is in the $y$ direction and the wave moves in the $x$ direction, $B$ is in the $z$ direction.

## Final answer

Direction:

$$+x$$

Wavelength:

$$\lambda=6.28\times10^{-7}\,m$$

Angular frequency:

$$\omega=3.0\times10^{15}\,rad/s$$

Magnetic field equation:

$$B_z(x,t)=3.33\times10^{-7}\sin(10^7x-3.0\times10^{15}t)\,T$$
