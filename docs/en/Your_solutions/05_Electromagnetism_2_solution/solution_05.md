# 5. Energy Stored by Charge in a Capacitor

## Useful definitions and formulas

Capacitance of a parallel-plate capacitor:

$$C=\varepsilon_0\frac{S}{d}$$

Energy stored in capacitor:

$$W=\frac{1}{2}CU^2$$

Electric field between plates:

$$E=\frac{U}{d}$$

Force of attraction between plates:

$$F=\frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0E^2S$$

where:

$S$ — plate area  
$d$ — distance between plates  
$U$ — voltage  
$\varepsilon_0=8.85\times10^{-12}$  

## Step-by-step solution

Given:

$$S=0.02\,m^2$$

$$d=5\,mm=0.005\,m$$

$$U=500\,V$$

### 1. Capacitance

$$C=8.85\times10^{-12}\frac{0.02}{0.005}$$

$$C=3.54\times10^{-11}\,F$$

### 2. Energy stored

$$W=\frac{1}{2}CU^2$$

$$W=\frac{1}{2}\cdot3.54\times10^{-11}\cdot500^2$$

$$W=4.43\times10^{-6}\,J$$

### 3. Electric field intensity

$$E=\frac{500}{0.005}$$

$$E=1.0\times10^5\,V/m$$

### 4. Force of attraction

$$F=\frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0E^2S$$

$$F=\frac{1}{2}\cdot8.85\times10^{-12}\cdot(1.0\times10^5)^2\cdot0.02$$

$$F=8.85\times10^{-4}\,N$$

## Final answer

$$C=3.54\times10^{-11}\,F$$

$$W=4.43\times10^{-6}\,J$$

$$E=1.0\times10^5\,V/m$$

$$F=8.85\times10^{-4}\,N$$
