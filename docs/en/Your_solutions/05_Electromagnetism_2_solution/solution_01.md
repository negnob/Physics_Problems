## 1. Gauss's Law

### Useful definitions and formulas

Electric flux $\Phi_E$ shows how much electric field passes through a surface.

Gauss's Law:

$$\Phi_E=\frac{q}{\varepsilon_0}$$

where:

$q$ — charge inside the closed surface, in coulombs $C$

$\varepsilon_0$ — electric constant:

$$\varepsilon_0=8.85\times10^{-12}\,\frac{C^2}{N\cdot m^2}$$

Important idea:  
The radius of the sphere does not matter here, because the charge is exactly inside a closed surface.

---

### Given

$$q=+2\,C$$

$$r=1\,m$$

$$\varepsilon_0=8.85\times10^{-12}\,\frac{C^2}{N\cdot m^2}$$

---

### Step-by-step solution

We use Gauss's Law:

$$\Phi_E=\frac{q}{\varepsilon_0}$$

Substitute the values:

$$\Phi_E=\frac{2}{8.85\times10^{-12}}$$

Now calculate:

$$\Phi_E\approx2.26\times10^{11}\,\frac{N\cdot m^2}{C}$$

The charge is positive, so the electric field goes outward from the charge.  
That means the electric flux is also positive and directed outward through the spherical surface.

---

### Final answer

$$\boxed{\Phi_E\approx2.26\times10^{11}\,\frac{N\cdot m^2}{C}}$$

The electric flux through the spherical surface is approximately:

$$2.26\times10^{11}\,\frac{N\cdot m^2}{C}$$

Direction: outward from the sphere.
