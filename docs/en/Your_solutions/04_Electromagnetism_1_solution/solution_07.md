## 7. Cyclotron Motion

### Useful definitions and formulas

When an electron is accelerated through a potential difference, electric potential energy becomes kinetic energy:

$$qV=\frac12mv^2$$

So:

$$v=\sqrt{\frac{2qV}{m}}$$

When the electron enters a magnetic field perpendicular to velocity, it moves in a circle.

Magnetic force provides centripetal force:

$$qvB=\frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Solving for radius:

$$r=\frac{mv}{qB}$$

A useful combined formula is:

$$r=\frac{\sqrt{2m qV}}{qB}$$

For an electron:

$$q=e=1.6\times10^{-19}\text{ C}$$

$$m_e=9.11\times10^{-31}\text{ kg}$$

---

### Step-by-step solution

Given:

$$V=5000\text{ V}$$

$$B=0.1\text{ T}$$

$$q=1.6\times10^{-19}\text{ C}$$

$$m=9.11\times10^{-31}\text{ kg}$$

Use:

$$r=\frac{\sqrt{2m qV}}{qB}$$

Substitute:

$$r=\frac{\sqrt{2(9.11\times10^{-31})(1.6\times10^{-19})(5000)}}{(1.6\times10^{-19})(0.1)}$$

Calculate:

$$r\approx2.39\times10^{-3}\text{ m}$$

Convert to millimeters:

$$r\approx2.39\text{ mm}$$

---

### Final answer

$$r\approx2.39\times10^{-3}\text{ m}$$

or

$$r\approx2.39\text{ mm}$$

The electron follows a circular path with radius about $2.39\text{ mm}$.
