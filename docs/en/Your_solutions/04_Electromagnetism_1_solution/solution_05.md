## 5. Field Levitation

### Useful definitions and formulas

Electric force on a charge in an electric field:

$$F_e=qE$$

Gravitational force:

$$F_g=mg$$

For levitation, the electric force must balance gravity:

$$qE=mg$$

So:

$$E=\frac{mg}{q}$$

For a proton:

$$q=e$$

---

### Step-by-step solution

We are given:

$$m_p=1.67\times10^{-27}\text{ kg}$$

$$e=1.6\times10^{-19}\text{ C}$$

$$g=9.8\text{ m/s}^2$$

Use:

$$E=\frac{mg}{q}$$

Substitute:

$$E=\frac{(1.67\times10^{-27})(9.8)}{1.6\times10^{-19}}$$

Calculate the numerator:

$$1.67\times10^{-27}\cdot9.8=1.6366\times10^{-26}$$

Now divide:

$$E=\frac{1.6366\times10^{-26}}{1.6\times10^{-19}}$$

$$E\approx1.02\times10^{-7}\text{ N/C}$$

---

### Direction

A proton has positive charge.

To make it levitate, the electric force must point upward.

For a positive charge, the electric force is in the same direction as the electric field.

So the electric field must point upward.

---

### Final answer

$$E\approx1.02\times10^{-7}\text{ N/C}$$

The electric field must be directed **upward**.
