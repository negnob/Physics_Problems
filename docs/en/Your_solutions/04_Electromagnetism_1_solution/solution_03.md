## 3. Electrostatic Equilibrium
---

Find the equilibrium position for a charge $q_3 = +1\text{C}$ placed on the line between a charge $q_1 = +4\text{C}$ and a charge $q_2 = +9\text{C}$, which are separated by a distance of 2 m.

---

### Useful definitions and formulas

For equilibrium, the net force on $q_3$ must be zero.

Coulomb force:

$$F=k\frac{|q_1q_3|}{r^2}$$

The charge $q_3$ is positive, and both $q_1$ and $q_2$ are also positive.

So $q_1$ repels $q_3$ to the right, and $q_2$ repels $q_3$ to the left.

At equilibrium:

$$F_{13}=F_{23}$$

---

### Step-by-step solution

Let $q_1=+4\text{ C}$ be at $x=0$.

Let $q_2=+9\text{ C}$ be at $x=2\text{ m}$.

Let $q_3$ be at distance $x$ from $q_1$.

Then the distance from $q_3$ to $q_2$ is:

$$2-x$$

At equilibrium:

$$k\frac{|q_1q_3|}{x^2}=k\frac{|q_2q_3|}{(2-x)^2}$$

Cancel $k$ and $q_3$:

$$\frac{q_1}{x^2}=\frac{q_2}{(2-x)^2}$$

Substitute charges:

$$\frac{4}{x^2}=\frac{9}{(2-x)^2}$$

Take square root:

$$\frac{2}{x}=\frac{3}{2-x}$$

Cross multiply:

$$2(2-x)=3x$$

$$4-2x=3x$$

$$4=5x$$

$$x=0.8\text{ m}$$

---

### Final answer

$$x=0.8\text{ m}$$

The equilibrium position is **0.8 m from the $+4\text{ C}$ charge** and **1.2 m from the $+9\text{ C}$ charge**.

It is closer to the smaller charge because the larger $+9\text{ C}$ charge pushes more strongly.
