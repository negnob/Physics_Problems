## 9. Vector Lorentz Force

### Useful definitions and formulas

The magnetic force on a moving charge is:

$$\vec F=q(\vec v\times\vec B)$$

The magnitude is:

$$F=q|\vec v\times\vec B|$$

For vectors:

$$\vec v=(2,-4,1)$$

$$\vec B=(1,2,-1)$$

The cross product is calculated using the determinant:

$$\vec v\times\vec B=
\begin{vmatrix}
\hat i & \hat j & \hat k\\
2 & -4 & 1\\
1 & 2 & -1
\end{vmatrix}
$$

For a proton:

$$q=e=1.6\times10^{-19}\text{ C}$$

---

### Step-by-step solution

Calculate the cross product:

$$\vec v\times\vec B=
\hat i((-4)(-1)-(1)(2))-\hat j((2)(-1)-(1)(1))+\hat k((2)(2)-(-4)(1))$$

Now simplify each component.

For $\hat i$:

$$(-4)(-1)-(1)(2)=4-2=2$$

For $\hat j$:

$$-[(2)(-1)-(1)(1)]=-[-2-1]=3$$

For $\hat k$:

$$(2)(2)-(-4)(1)=4+4=8$$

So:

$$\vec v\times\vec B=2\hat i+3\hat j+8\hat k$$

Now find the magnitude:

$$|\vec v\times\vec B|=\sqrt{2^2+3^2+8^2}$$

$$|\vec v\times\vec B|=\sqrt{4+9+64}$$

$$|\vec v\times\vec B|=\sqrt{77}$$

$$|\vec v\times\vec B|\approx8.775$$

Now multiply by proton charge:

$$F=q|\vec v\times\vec B|$$

$$F=(1.6\times10^{-19})(8.775)$$

$$F\approx1.40\times10^{-18}\text{ N}$$

---

### Final answer

$$F\approx1.40\times10^{-18}\text{ N}$$
