## 4. Force Comparison

### Useful definitions and formulas

Electric force between electron and proton:

$$F_e=k\frac{e^2}{r^2}$$

Gravitational force between electron and proton:

$$F_g=G\frac{m_em_p}{r^2}$$

Constants:

$$k=8.99\times10^9$$

$$e=1.6\times10^{-19}\text{ C}$$

$$G=6.67\times10^{-11}$$

$$m_e=9.11\times10^{-31}\text{ kg}$$

$$m_p=1.67\times10^{-27}\text{ kg}$$

$$r=5.3\times10^{-11}\text{ m}$$

---

### Step-by-step solution

### 1) Electric force

Use:

$$F_e=k\frac{e^2}{r^2}$$

Substitute:

$$F_e=8.99\times10^9\frac{(1.6\times10^{-19})^2}{(5.3\times10^{-11})^2}$$

Calculate:

$$F_e\approx8.2\times10^{-8}\text{ N}$$

---

### 2) Gravitational force

Use:

$$F_g=G\frac{m_em_p}{r^2}$$

Substitute:

$$F_g=6.67\times10^{-11}\frac{(9.11\times10^{-31})(1.67\times10^{-27})}{(5.3\times10^{-11})^2}$$

Calculate:

$$F_g\approx3.6\times10^{-47}\text{ N}$$

---

### 3) Ratio

Use:

$$\frac{F_e}{F_g}=\frac{8.2\times10^{-8}}{3.6\times10^{-47}}$$

$$\frac{F_e}{F_g}\approx2.3\times10^{39}$$

---

### Final answer

$$F_e\approx8.2\times10^{-8}\text{ N}$$

$$F_g\approx3.6\times10^{-47}\text{ N}$$

$$\frac{F_e}{F_g}\approx2.3\times10^{39}$$

The electric force is enormously stronger than gravity.
