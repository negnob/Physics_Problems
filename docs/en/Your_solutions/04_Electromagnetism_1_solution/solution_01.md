## 1. Coulomb's Law

### Useful definitions and formulas

Coulomb's law:

$$F=k\frac{|q_1q_2|}{r^2}$$

where:

- $F$ is the electric force
- $k=8.99\times10^9\text{ N m}^2/\text{C}^2$
- $q_1,q_2$ are charges
- $r$ is the distance between charges

The force is attractive if the charges have opposite signs.

---

### Step-by-step solution

Four charges of $+1.0\text{ C}$ are placed at the corners of a square.

A charge $q_0=-2.0\text{ C}$ is placed at the center.

Each positive corner charge attracts the negative center charge.

Because the square is symmetric, the forces from opposite corners have the same magnitude but opposite directions.

So:

- force from top-left cancels force from bottom-right
- force from top-right cancels force from bottom-left

Therefore, the total electric force at the center is zero.

---

### Final answer

$$F_{\text{net}}=0\text{ N}$$

The direction is undefined because the net force is zero.

The center charge feels equal attraction in all diagonal directions, so everything cancels.
