## 10. Lorentz Force Acting on Wire

### Useful definitions and formulas

The magnetic force on a current-carrying wire is:

$$F=BIL\sin\theta$$

where:

- $F$ is the magnetic force
- $B$ is magnetic field
- $I$ is current
- $L$ is length of wire
- $\theta$ is the angle between the wire and the magnetic field

Given:

$$B=0.5\text{ T}$$

$$I=10\text{ A}$$

$$L=2.0\text{ m}$$

First calculate the constant part:

$$BIL=(0.5)(10)(2.0)=10\text{ N}$$

So:

$$F=10\sin\theta$$

---

### a) Angle $90^\circ$

Use:

$$F=10\sin90^\circ$$

Since:

$$\sin90^\circ=1$$

Then:

$$F=10\cdot1=10\text{ N}$$

---

### b) Angle $45^\circ$

Use:

$$F=10\sin45^\circ$$

Since:

$$\sin45^\circ=\frac{\sqrt2}{2}\approx0.707$$

Then:

$$F=10\cdot0.707$$

$$F\approx7.07\text{ N}$$

---

### c) Angle $0^\circ$

Use:

$$F=10\sin0^\circ$$

Since:

$$\sin0^\circ=0$$

Then:

$$F=10\cdot0=0\text{ N}$$

---

### Final answer

a) $$F=10\text{ N}$$

b) $$F\approx7.07\text{ N}$$

c) $$F=0\text{ N}$$

The force is maximum at $90^\circ$ and zero when the wire is parallel to the magnetic field.
