## 2. Electric Potential

### Useful definitions and formulas

Electric potential from one point charge is:

$$V=k\frac{q}{r}$$

For many charges, electric potential is scalar, so we add values directly:

$$V_{\text{total}}=k\sum\frac{q_i}{r_i}$$

where:

- $V$ is electric potential
- $k=8.99\times10^9\text{ N m}^2/\text{C}^2$
- $q_i$ are charges
- $r_i$ are distances from charges to the point

---

### Step-by-step solution

The charges are placed at the corners of a square:

$$+1\text{ C},-2\text{ C},+3\text{ C},-4\text{ C}$$

The point is the center of the square.

All corners are the same distance from the center, so all $r_i$ are equal.

Therefore:

$$V=k\frac{q_1+q_2+q_3+q_4}{r}$$

Now add the charges:

$$q_{\text{total}}=1-2+3-4$$

$$q_{\text{total}}=-2\text{ C}$$

Now find the distance from the center to a corner.

The side of the square is:

$$a=1.0\text{ m}$$

The diagonal is:

$$d=a\sqrt2=\sqrt2\text{ m}$$

The distance from center to corner is half the diagonal:

$$r=\frac{\sqrt2}{2}=0.707\text{ m}$$

Now substitute:

$$V=8.99\times10^9\frac{-2}{0.707}$$

$$V\approx-2.54\times10^{10}\text{ V}$$

---

### Final answer

$$V\approx-2.54\times10^{10}\text{ V}$$

The potential is negative because the total charge contribution is negative.
