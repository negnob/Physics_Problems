## 8. Lorentz Force

### Useful definitions and formulas

The magnetic part of the Lorentz force is:

$$F=qvB\sin\theta$$

where:

- $F$ is magnetic force
- $q$ is charge
- $v$ is speed
- $B$ is magnetic field
- $\theta$ is the angle between velocity and magnetic field

If velocity is perpendicular to the magnetic field:

$$\theta=90^\circ$$

and:

$$\sin90^\circ=1$$

So:

$$F=qvB$$

---

### Step-by-step solution

Given:

$$q=2\times10^{-19}\text{ C}$$

$$v=10^6\text{ m/s}$$

$$B=0.5\text{ T}$$

Since the velocity is perpendicular to the magnetic field:

$$F=qvB$$

Substitute:

$$F=(2\times10^{-19})(10^6)(0.5)$$

First multiply:

$$2\times0.5=1$$

So:

$$F=1\times10^{-19}\times10^6$$

$$F=1\times10^{-13}\text{ N}$$

---

### Final answer

$$F=1.0\times10^{-13}\text{ N}$$
