# 2. Resistors

## Useful definitions and formulas

### Series connection

When resistors are connected in series:

$$R_{eq}=R_1+R_2+R_3$$

---

### Parallel connection

When resistors are connected in parallel:

$$\frac{1}{R_{eq}}=\frac{1}{R_1}+\frac{1}{R_2}+\frac{1}{R_3}$$

---

All resistors have:

$$R=1\,\Omega$$

We must list all unique equivalent resistances possible using exactly three resistors.

---

# Step-by-step solution

## 1. All resistors in series

$$R_{eq}=1+1+1$$

$$R_{eq}=3\,\Omega$$

---

## 2. All resistors in parallel

$$\frac{1}{R_{eq}}=1+1+1=3$$

$$R_{eq}=\frac{1}{3}\,\Omega$$

---

## 3. Two resistors in series, then parallel with the third

First combine two in series:

$$R=1+1=2\,\Omega$$

Now place this in parallel with $1\,\Omega$:

$$\frac{1}{R_{eq}}=\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{1}$$

$$\frac{1}{R_{eq}}=\frac{3}{2}$$

$$R_{eq}=\frac{2}{3}\,\Omega$$

---

## 4. Two resistors in parallel, then series with the third

First combine two in parallel:

$$\frac{1}{R}=\frac{1}{1}+\frac{1}{1}=2$$

$$R=\frac{1}{2}\,\Omega$$

Now connect in series with the third resistor:

$$R_{eq}=\frac{1}{2}+1$$

$$R_{eq}=\frac{3}{2}\,\Omega$$

---

# Final answer

All unique equivalent resistances are:

$$\frac{1}{3}\,\Omega$$

$$\frac{2}{3}\,\Omega$$

$$\frac{3}{2}\,\Omega$$

$$3\,\Omega$$
