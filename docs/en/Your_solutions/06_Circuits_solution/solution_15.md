# 15. Resistor Cube

## Useful definitions and formulas

A cube has 12 equal resistors, each with resistance:

$$R$$

For a cube made of identical resistors, the equivalent resistance between two opposite corners is a standard symmetry result:

$$R_{eq}=\frac{5}{6}R$$

---

## Step-by-step solution

Because the cube is symmetric, the three vertices connected directly to one corner have the same electric potential.

Also, the three vertices connected directly to the opposite corner have the same electric potential.

This lets us combine groups of resistors.

From the first corner, current splits equally through three resistors:

$$R_1=\frac{R}{3}$$

In the middle section, there are six resistors connecting the two equal-potential groups:

$$R_2=\frac{R}{6}$$

Near the opposite corner, current combines through three resistors:

$$R_3=\frac{R}{3}$$

These three equivalent parts are in series:

$$R_{eq}=\frac{R}{3}+\frac{R}{6}+\frac{R}{3}$$

$$R_{eq}=\frac{2R}{6}+\frac{R}{6}+\frac{2R}{6}$$

$$R_{eq}=\frac{5R}{6}$$

---

## Final answer

$$R_{eq}=\frac{5}{6}R$$
