# 7. Capacitors in Parallel
Two capacitors, $C_1=4\,\mu\text{F}$ and $C_2=6\,\mu\text{F}$, are connected in parallel to a 10 V battery. What is the total charge stored on the capacitors? What is the total energy stored?

---

## Useful definitions and formulas

For capacitors connected in parallel:

$$C_{eq}=C_1+C_2$$

Charge stored:

$$Q=CV$$

Energy stored in a capacitor:

$$W=\frac{1}{2}CV^2$$

where:

$C$ — capacitance  
$V$ — voltage  
$Q$ — charge  
$W$ — energy  

---

## Step-by-step solution

Given:

$$C_1=4\,\mu F$$

$$C_2=6\,\mu F$$

$$V=10\,V$$

---

## Step 1 — Equivalent capacitance

Since the capacitors are connected in parallel:

$$C_{eq}=4+6$$

$$C_{eq}=10\,\mu F$$

Convert to farads:

$$C_{eq}=10\times10^{-6}\,F$$

---

## Step 2 — Total charge stored

Using:

$$Q=CV$$

$$Q=(10\times10^{-6})\cdot10$$

$$Q=100\times10^{-6}\,C$$

$$Q=1.0\times10^{-4}\,C$$

or:

$$Q=100\,\mu C$$

---

## Step 3 — Total energy stored

Using:

$$W=\frac{1}{2}CV^2$$

$$W=\frac{1}{2}(10\times10^{-6})(10)^2$$

$$W=\frac{1}{2}(10\times10^{-6})(100)$$

$$W=5\times10^{-4}\,J$$

---

## Final answer

Total charge:

$$Q=1.0\times10^{-4}\,C$$

or

$$Q=100\,\mu C$$

Total energy stored:

$$W=5\times10^{-4}\,J$$
