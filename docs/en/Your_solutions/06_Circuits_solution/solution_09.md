# 9. Current

Charge flowing through the wire is given by the function of time $Q(t)=5t^2+5$. What is the current at $t=3$ s?

## Useful definitions and formulas

Current is the rate of change of charge:

$$I(t)=\frac{dQ}{dt}$$

where:

$Q$ — charge  
$I$ — current  
$t$ — time  

---

## Step-by-step solution

We need to find the current at:

$$t=3s$$

The current is found by taking the derivative of the charge function with respect to time.

Given:

$$Q(t)=5t^2+5$$

Now differentiate the function step by step.

---

Take the derivative of:

$$5t^2$$

Using the power rule:

- multiply the coefficient by the exponent
- decrease the exponent by 1

So:

$$5\cdot2=10$$

and:

$$t^2\rightarrow t^{2-1}=t$$

Therefore:

$$\frac{d}{dt}(5t^2)=10t$$

---

Now take the derivative of the constant:

$$\frac{d}{dt}(5)=0$$

because a constant number does not change with time.

---

Now combine both parts:

$$I(t)=10t+0$$

$$I(t)=10t$$

---

Now substitute:

$$t=3s$$

$$I(3)=10\cdot3$$

$$I(3)=30A$$

---

## Final answer

$$I=30A$$
