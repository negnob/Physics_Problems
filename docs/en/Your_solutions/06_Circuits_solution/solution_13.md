# 13. Transformer Ratio

## Useful definitions and formulas

For an ideal transformer:

$$\frac{V_s}{V_p}=\frac{N_s}{N_p}$$

where:

$V_p$ — primary voltage  
$V_s$ — secondary voltage  
$N_p$ — primary turns  
$N_s$ — secondary turns  

---

## Step-by-step solution

Given:

$$V_p=120\,V$$

$$V_s=9.0\,V$$

$$N_p=400$$

Use the transformer formula:

$$\frac{9.0}{120}=\frac{N_s}{400}$$

Solve for $N_s$:

$$N_s=400\cdot\frac{9.0}{120}$$

$$N_s=30$$

---

## Final answer

$$N_s=30\,turns$$
