# 6. Kirchhoff's Laws Again
Calculate the current flowing through the ammeter.

## Useful definitions and formulas

Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law:

$$\sum V=0$$

Ohm’s Law:

$$V=IR$$

For resistors in series:

$$R_{eq}=R_1+R_2+...$$

---

## Step-by-step solution

Given:

$$R_1=10\,\Omega$$

$$R_2=20\,\Omega$$

Internal resistances:

$$r_w=1\,\Omega$$

Sources:

$$\mathcal{E}_1=9\,V$$

$$\mathcal{E}_2=4.5\,V$$

---

## Step 1 — Total resistance

All resistors are connected in one loop, so they are in series.

Total resistance:

$$R_{total}=10+20+1+1$$

$$R_{total}=32\,\Omega$$

---

## Step 2 — Total electromotive force

The batteries oppose each other because their polarities are opposite in the loop.

Therefore:

$$\mathcal{E}_{total}=9-4.5$$

$$\mathcal{E}_{total}=4.5\,V$$

---

## Step 3 — Calculate current

Using Ohm’s Law:

$$I=\frac{\mathcal{E}_{total}}{R_{total}}$$

$$I=\frac{4.5}{32}$$

$$I=0.141\,A$$

---

## Final answer

The current flowing through the ammeter is:

$$I=0.141\,A$$
