# 12. Transformer Currents

## Useful definitions and formulas

For an ideal transformer:

$$\frac{V_s}{V_p}=\frac{N_s}{N_p}$$

Also, power is conserved:

$$V_p I_p=V_s I_s$$

where:

$V_p$ ‚Äî primary voltage  
$V_s$ ‚Äî secondary voltage  
$N_p$ ‚Äî primary turns  
$N_s$ ‚Äî secondary turns  
$I_p$ ‚Äî primary current  
$I_s$ ‚Äî secondary current  

---

## Step-by-step solution

Given:

$$N_p=1000$$

$$N_s=200$$

$$V_p=120\,V$$

$$I_s=3\,A$$

### Secondary voltage

$$\frac{V_s}{120}=\frac{200}{1000}$$

$$V_s=120\cdot0.2$$

$$V_s=24\,V$$

### Primary current

Using power conservation:

$$V_pI_p=V_sI_s$$

$$120I_p=24\cdot3$$

$$120I_p=72$$

$$I_p=\frac{72}{120}$$

$$I_p=0.6\,A$$

---

## Final answer

Secondary voltage:

$$V_s=24\,V$$

Primary current:

$$I_p=0.6\,A$$
