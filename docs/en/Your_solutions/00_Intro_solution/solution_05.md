## 5. Trigonometry

A vector $\vec{A}$ has magnitude $15$ and makes an angle of $\theta=60^\circ$ with the horizontal axis. We need to calculate its horizontal and vertical components.

---

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

### 1. Vector components
A vector can be split into:
- a horizontal component
- a vertical component

If the angle is measured from the horizontal axis, then:

$$A_x=A\cos\theta$$

$$A_y=A\sin\theta$$

where:
- $A$ is the magnitude of the vector
- $A_x$ is the horizontal component
- $A_y$ is the vertical component

---

### 2. Trigonometric values for $60^\circ$

$$\cos60^\circ=\frac{1}{2}$$

$$\sin60^\circ=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

We are given:

$$A=15$$

$$\theta=60^\circ$$

---

### Step 1. Find the horizontal component

Use the formula

$$A_x=A\cos\theta$$

Substitute the values:

$$A_x=15\cos60^\circ$$

Since

$$\cos60^\circ=\frac{1}{2}$$

we get

$$A_x=15\cdot\frac{1}{2}$$

$$A_x=7.5$$

---

### Step 2. Find the vertical component

Use the formula

$$A_y=A\sin\theta$$

Substitute the values:

$$A_y=15\sin60^\circ$$

Since

$$\sin60^\circ=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$$

we get

$$A_y=15\cdot\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$$

$$A_y=\frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}$$

Approximate value:

$$A_y\approx12.99$$

---

## Final Answer

The horizontal component is

$$\boxed{A_x=7.5}$$

The vertical component is

$$\boxed{A_y=\frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}\approx12.99}$$
