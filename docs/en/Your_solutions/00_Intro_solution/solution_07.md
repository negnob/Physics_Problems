## 7. Logic & Series

A bicycle is $10$ meters from a wall and moves toward it at a constant speed of $1\text{ m/s}$. A fly starts from the bicycle's front wheel and flies toward the wall at $2\text{ m/s}$. Each time it hits the wall, it instantly turns back and flies to the bicycle, and so on.

We need to find the total distance the fly travels before the bicycle reaches the wall and the fly is crushed.

---

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

### 1. Distance formula
For constant speed motion, the distance is

$$\text{distance}=\text{speed}\cdot\text{time}$$

This is the most important formula here.

---

### 2. Key idea of the problem
This problem looks like it needs an infinite series, because the fly goes back and forth infinitely many times.

But there is a much smarter way:

- find how long the bicycle takes to reach the wall
- during that whole time, the fly is always moving at constant speed
- then use

$$\text{distance}=\text{speed}\cdot\text{time}$$

So we do not actually need to sum all the little trips one by one. Infinite trips, same final answer. Math loves these little scams.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1. Find how long the bicycle takes to reach the wall

The bicycle starts $10$ meters from the wall and moves at

$$1\text{ m/s}$$

Using

$$\text{time}=\frac{\text{distance}}{\text{speed}}$$

we get

$$t=\frac{10}{1}=10\text{ s}$$

So the bicycle reaches the wall in

$$10\text{ seconds}$$

---

### Step 2. Find how far the fly travels in that time

The fly moves continuously at

$$2\text{ m/s}$$

for the whole

$$10\text{ s}$$

Using

$$\text{distance}=\text{speed}\cdot\text{time}$$

we get

$$d=2\cdot10=20\text{ m}$$

---

## Final Answer

The total distance traveled by the fly before being crushed is

$$\boxed{20\text{ m}}$$
