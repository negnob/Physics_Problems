# Systems of Equations

## Useful definitions & formulas

### 1) System of linear equations
A **system of equations** is a set of two or more equations with the same variables.  
A **solution** is the pair of values that satisfies **all equations simultaneously**.

Example form:

\[
a1x + b1y = c1
\]

\[
a2x + b2y = c2
\]

---

### 2) Substitution Method
Steps:
1. Solve one equation for one variable.
2. Substitute this expression into the other equation.
3. Solve for the remaining variable.
4. Substitute back to find the second variable.

This method is usually easiest when one equation already isolates a variable.

---

## Given system

\[
2x + 3y = 12
\]

\[
x - y = 1
\]

---

# Step-by-step solution

## Step 1 — Express one variable from the second equation

From

\[
x - y = 1
\]

solve for \(x\):

\[
x = y + 1
\]

---

## Step 2 — Substitute into the first equation

Now substitute \(x = y + 1\) into

\[
2x + 3y = 12
\]

\[
2(y + 1) + 3y = 12
\]

---

## Step 3 — Simplify the equation

Expand:

\[
2y + 2 + 3y = 12
\]

Combine like terms:

\[
5y + 2 = 12
\]

---

## Step 4 — Solve for \(y\)

\[
5y = 10
\]

\[
y = 2
\]

---

## Step 5 — Substitute back to find \(x\)

Use:

\[
x = y + 1
\]

\[
x = 2 + 1
\]

\[
x = 3
\]

---

# Final Answer

\[
x = 3
\]

\[
y = 2
\]

---

## Quick check (always good practice)

First equation:

\[
2(3) + 3(2) = 6 + 6 = 12
\]

Second equation:

\[
3 - 2 = 1
\]

Both equations are satisfied ✔
