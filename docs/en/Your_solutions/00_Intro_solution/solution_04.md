## 4. Rearranging Formulas

The formula for the period of a simple pendulum is

$$T=2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

We need to rearrange this formula to make $g$ the subject.

---

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

### 1. Rearranging a formula
To rearrange a formula means to isolate the required variable on one side of the equation.

Here we want to isolate $g$.

---

### 2. Inverse operations
When rearranging equations, we use inverse operations:
- addition ↔ subtraction
- multiplication ↔ division
- square root ↔ squaring

Since the formula contains a square root, we will need to square both sides at one step.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

We start with

$$T=2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

---

### Step 1. Divide both sides by $2\pi$

$$\frac{T}{2\pi}=\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

This removes the coefficient $2\pi$ from the right side.

---

### Step 2. Square both sides

To remove the square root, square both sides:

$$\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2=\frac{L}{g}$$

---

### Step 3. Rewrite the left side

$$\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2}=\frac{L}{g}$$

---

### Step 4. Solve for $g$

Now multiply both sides by $g$:

$$g\cdot\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2}=L$$

Now divide both sides by $\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2}$:

$$g=\frac{L}{\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2}}$$

Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal:

$$g=L\cdot\frac{4\pi^2}{T^2}$$

So

$$g=\frac{4\pi^2L}{T^2}$$

---

## Final Answer

$$\boxed{g=\frac{4\pi^2L}{T^2}}$$
